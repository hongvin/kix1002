# Tutorial 3 & 4: 2nd Order Ordinary Differential Equations

1. Use the Wronskian to show whether the give set of functions is linearly dependent or linearly independent.

    a. $y_1=x \qquad y_2=x+1$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&=x, \qquad &y'_1&=1\\
    y_2&=x+1, \qquad &y'_2&=1
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    W(y_1,y_2)&=\left| \begin{array}{cc}x&x+1\\1&1\end{array} \right|\\
    &=x-(x-1)\\
    &=1 \neq 0
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Since $W(x,x+1)\neq 0$, thus $\{ x, x+1 \}$ are linearly independent

    :::

    b. $y_1=e^{\alpha x}\sin\beta x \qquad y_2=e^{\alpha x}\cos\beta x$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&=e^{\alpha x}\sin\beta x, \qquad &y'_1&=\beta e^{\alpha x}\cos\beta x+ \alpha e^{\alpha x}\sin \beta x\\
    y_2&=e^{\alpha x}\cos\beta, \qquad &y'_2&=-\beta e^{\alpha x}\sin\beta x+ \alpha e^{\alpha x}\cos \beta x
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    W(y_1,y_2)&=\left| \begin{array}{cc}e^{\alpha x}\sin\beta x&e^{\alpha x}\cos\beta x\\\beta e^{\alpha x}\cos\beta x+ \alpha e^{\alpha x}\sin \beta x&-\beta e^{\alpha x}\sin\beta x+ \alpha e^{\alpha x}\cos \beta x\end{array} \right|\\
    &=-\beta e^{2\alpha x}\sin^2 \beta x+\alpha e^{2\alpha x}\sin\beta x\cos\beta x - \beta e^{2\alpha x}\cos^2\beta x - \alpha e^{2\alpha x}\sin\beta x\cos\beta x\\
    &=-\beta e^{2\alpha x}(sin^2\beta x+\cos^2\beta x)\\
    &=-\beta e^{2\alpha x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Since $\beta\neq0$ and $e^{2\alpha x}\neq 0$, thus $W(y_1,y_2)\neq 0$. 
    
    $\therefore$ Linearly independent.

    :::

2. Verify that each of the given functions is a solution of the differential equation, and use their Wronskian to show that these solutions are linearly independent.
    
    Verify the linear combination of the solutions is also a solution.

    a.  $y_1=e^x\qquad y_2=e^{-2x}\qquad y''+y'-2y=0$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&=e^x,\qquad &y_1'&=e^x\qquad &y_1''&=e^x\\
    y_2&=e^{-2x}\qquad &y_2'&=-2e^{-2x}\qquad &y_2''&=4e^{-2x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    For

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&\rightarrow e^x+e^x-2e^x=0\\
    y_2&\rightarrow 4e^{-2x}+(-2e^{-2x})-2(e^{-2x})=0
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Both are solution.

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    W(y_1,y_2)&=\left| \begin{array}{cc}e^x&e^{-2x}\\e^x&-2e^{-2x}\end{array} \right|\\
    &=-2e^{-x}-e^{-x}\\
    &=-3e^{-x}\neq 0
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Since $e^{-x}\neq0$, thus $W(y_1,y_2)\neq 0$. 
    
    $\therefore$ Linearly independent.

    :::

    b. $y_1=x^2\qquad y_2=x^{-1}\qquad x^2y''-2y=0$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&=x^2,\qquad &y_1'&=2x,\qquad &y_1''=2\\
    y_2&=x^{-1},\qquad &y_2'&=-x^{-2},\qquad &y_2''=-2x^{-3}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    For

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&\rightarrow x^2y''-2y=x^2(2)-2(x^2)=0\\
    y_2&\rightarrow x^2(2x^{-3})-2(x^{-1})=2x^{-1}-2x^{-1}=0
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Both are solution.

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    W(y_1,y_2)&=\left| \begin{array}{cc}x^2&x^{-1}\\2x&-x^{-2}\end{array} \right|\\
    &=-x^0-2x^0\\
    &=-3\neq 0
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    
    $\therefore$ Linearly independent.

    :::

    c. $y_1=e^{-x}\qquad y_2=xe^{-x}\qquad y''+2y'+y=0$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&=e^{-x} \qquad &y_1'&=-e^{-x} \qquad &y_1''&=e^{-x}\\
    y_2&=xe^{-x} \qquad &y_2'&=-xe^{-x}+e^{-x} \qquad &y_2''&=xe^{-x}-e^{-x}-e^{-x}=xe^{-x}-2e^{-x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    For

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y_1&\rightarrow y''+2y'+y=e^{-x}+2(-e^{-x})+e^{-x}=0\\
    y_2&\rightarrow (xe^{-x}-2e^{-x})+2(-xe^{-x}+e^{-x})+2e^{-x}=0
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Both are solution.

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    W(y_1,y_2)&=\left| \begin{array}{cc}e^{-x}&xe^{-x}\\-e^{-x}&xe^{-x}+e^{-x}\end{array} \right|\\
    &=(-xe^{-2x}+e^{-2x})+(xe^{-2x})\\
    &=e^{-2x}\neq 0
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    
    $\therefore$ Linearly independent.

    :::

3. Solve the following second order ODEs 

    a. $y''-4y=0$ 

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y''-4y&=0\\
    m^2-4&=0\\
    m&=\pm 2\\
    \Rightarrow y&=C_1e^{2x}+C_2 e^{-2x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    :::

    b. $y''-y'-6y=0$ 

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y''-y'+7y&=0\\
    m^2-m+6&=0\\
    (m-3)(m+2)&=0\\
    m&=3,-2\\
    \Rightarrow y&=C_1e^{3x}+C_2e^{-2x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    :::

    c. $6y''+y'-y=0$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    6y''+y'-y&=0\\
    6m^2+m-1&=0\\
    (3m-1)(2m+1)&=0\\
    m&=\frac{1}{3}, -\frac{1}{2}\\
    \Rightarrow y&=C_1e^{\frac{1}{3}x}+C_2e^{-\frac{1}{2}x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    :::

    d. $y''+y=0$ 

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y''+y&=0\\
    m^2+1&=0\\
    m&=\sqrt{-1}=\pm i \\
    y_1=C_1e^{ix} &, y_2=C_1e^{-ix}\\
    \Rightarrow y&=e^{ix}(C_1\cos x+C_2\sin x)\\
    &=C_1\cos x+C_2\sin x
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    :::
    
    e. $y''+4y'+8y=0$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y''+4y'+8y&=0\\
    m&2+4m+8&=0\\
    m&=\frac{-4\pm\sqrt{4^2-4(1)(8)}}{2(1)}\\
    &=\frac{1}{2}\left(-4\pm\sqrt{16-32}\right)\\
    &=\frac{1}{2}(-4\pm 4i)\\&=-2\pm 2i\\
    y_1=C_1e^{(-2+2i)x} &, y_2=C_2e^{(-2-2i)x} \\
    y&=e^{-2x}(C_1\cos 2x + C_2\sin 2x)
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    :::

4. Solve the following second order ODEs with initial-value conditions

    a. $y''-4y=0\qquad\qquad y(0)=4\qquad y'(0)=12$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y''-4y&=0\\m^2&=4\\m&=\pm2\\
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    When

    $$
    x=0, y=4 \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad 4=C_1+C_2 \tag{1}
    $$

    $$
    x=0, y'=12 \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad 12=2C_1-2C_2 \tag{2}
    $$

    Take $(2)+2\times(1)$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    (12+8)&=(2C_1+2C_2)+(2C_1-2C_2)\\
    20&=4C_1\\
    C_1&=5
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Into $(1)$,

    $$
    4=5+C_2 \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad C_2=-1
    $$

    Thus,

    $$
    y=5e^{2x}-e^{-2x}
    $$

    :::

    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}

    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Thus, 
    
    $$
    y=-1.4e^{-3x}+8.8xe^{-3x}
    $$

    :::

    c. $4y''-8y+3y=0 \qquad\qquad y(1)=\frac{4}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}} \qquad y'(1)=\frac{2}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}}$

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    4y''-8y'+3y&=0\\
    4m^2-8m+3&=0\\
    (2m-3)(2m-1)=0\\
    m&=\frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{2}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    General solution. $y=C_1e^{\frac{1}{2}x}+C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}x}$, $y'=\frac{1}{2}C_1e^{\frac{1}{2}x}+\frac{3}{2}C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}x}$

    When

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    x=1, y=\frac{4}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}} \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \frac{4}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}}&=C_1e^{\frac{1}{2}}+C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} \\
    \left(\frac{4}{5}-C_1\right)e^{\frac{1}{2}}&=C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} \tag{1}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    x=1, y'=\frac{2}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}} \qquad \Rightarrow  \qquad \frac{2}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}} &= \frac{1}{2}C_1e^{\frac{1}{2}}+\frac{3}{2}C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} \\
    \left(\frac{2}{5}-\frac{1}{2}C_1\right) e^{\frac{1}{2}} &=\frac{3}{2}C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} \\
    \frac{2}{3}\left(\frac{2}{5}-\frac{1}{2}C_1\right)e^{\frac{1}{2}} &= C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} \tag{2}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Let $(1) = (2)$,

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \left(\frac{4}{5}-C_1\right)e^{\frac{1}{2}}&=\left(\frac{4}{15}-\frac{1}{3}C_1\right)e^{\frac{1}{2}}\\
    \left(\frac{4}{5}-\frac{4}{15}\right)&=\left(1-\frac{1}{3}\right)C_1\\
    \frac{2}{3}C_1&=\frac{8}{15} \\
    C_1&=\frac{4}{5}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Into $(1)$,

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \left(\frac{4}{5}-\frac{4}{5}\right)e^{\frac{1}{2}}&=C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} \\
    C_2e^{\frac{3}{2}} &=0 \qquad e^{\frac{3}{2}} \neq 0 \\
    C_2&=0
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Thus,

    $$
    y=\frac{4}{5}e^{\frac{1}{2}x}
    $$

    :::

    :::info[Solution]

    \end{aligned}

    $$

    :::

    :::info[Solution]

    m^2+2m+2&=0\\

    \end{aligned}

    \end{aligned}
    $$

    When

    \end{aligned}

    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Thus,

    $$
    y=e^{-x}\cos x
    $$

    :::

6. Obtain a general solution for nonhomogeneous problems using the method of undetermined coefficients.

    a. $y''-2y'+5y=e^x\cos 2x$ 

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    m^2-2m+5&=0\\
    m&=\frac{1}{2}[2\pm\sqrt{4-20}]\\
    \frac{1}{2}(2\pm 4i)\\1\pm 2i \\ \\ 
    y_c&=e^x(C_1\cos 2x+C_2\sin 2x)\\
    \alpha&=(1+2i)\neq m_1\neq m_2
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Thus

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    g(x)&=e^x\cos 2x\\
    e^x\cdot \text{Re}e^{2ix}\\
    \text{Re}(e^{(1+2i)x})\\
    y_p&=A_x\text{Re}(e^{(1+2i)x})
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Let

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    Y_p&=Axe^{(1+2i)x},\qquad y_p=Ax\text{Re}(e^{(1+2i)x}) \\ 
    Y'_p&=(1+2i)Axe^{(1+2i)x}+Ae^{(1+2i)x} \\
    Y''_p&=(1+2i)^2Axe^{(1+2i)x}+(1+2i)Ae^{(1+2i)x}+(1+2i)Ae^{(1+2i)x}\\
    &=(-3+4i)Axe^{(1+2i)x}+2A(1+2i)e^{(1+2i)x}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    (-3+4i)Axe^{(1+2i)x}+2A(1+2i)e^{(1+2i)x}-2\left[(1+2i)Axe^{(1+2i)x}+Ae^{(1+2i)x}\right]+5[Axe^{(1+2i)x}]=e^x\cos 2x \\
    [-3+4i-2-4i+5]Axe^{(1+2i)x}+[2+4i-2]Ae^{(1+2i)x}&=e^x\cos 2x

    b. $y''+y=2x\sin x$ 

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    m^2&=-1 \\ 
    m&=\pm i \\ \\ 
    y_p&=C_1e^{-ix} + C_2e^{ix} \\ 
    &=C_1\cos x+C_2 \sin x
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Compare $e^{\alpha x}Q_n(x)=2x\sin x$,

    $$
    \alpha=1=m_1\neq m_2
    $$

    Let

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    Y_p&=(Ax^2+Bx)e^{ix}, \qquad y_p=\text{Im}Y_p \\ 
    Y'_p&=(2Ax+B)e^{ix}+i(Ax^2+Bx)e^{ix} \\
    Y''_p&=(2A)e^{ix}+i(2Ax+B)e^{ix}+i(2Ax+B)e^{ix}+i^2(Ax^2+Bx)e^{ix} \\ 
    &=(2A)e^{ix}+2i(2Ax+B)e^{ix}-(Ax^2+Bx)e^{ix}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \left[(2Ae^{ix})+2i(2Ax+B)e^{ix}-(Ax+B)e^{ix}\right]+(Ax+B)e^{ix}&=2xe^{ix} \\ 
    2A+4Aix+2Bi&=2x \\ 
    (4Ai)x+(2A+2Bi)&=2x
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Thus

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    4Ai&=2 \qquad &(2A+2Bi)&=0 \\ 
    A&=\frac{1}{2i}=-\frac{1}{2}i &B&=-\frac{A}{i}=-\frac{(\frac{1}{2}i)}{i}=\frac{1}{2}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    Into equation

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    Y_p&=\left(-\frac{1}{2}ix^2+\frac{1}{2}x\right)e^{ix} \\
    &=\left(-\frac{1}{2}ix^2+\frac{1}{2}x\right)(\cos x+i\sin x) \\
    &=-\frac{1}{2}ix^2\cos x+\frac{1}{2}x^2\sin x+\frac{1}{2}x\cos x+\frac{1}{2} ix \sin x \\
    &=\left(\frac{1}{2}x^2\sin x+\frac{1}{2}x\cos x\right)+i\left(-\frac{1}{2}x^2\cos x+\frac{1}{2}x \sin x\right) \\ \\
    y_p&=\text{Im}Y_p \\ 
    &=-\frac{1}{2}x^2\cos x+\frac{1}{2}x\sin x
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \therefore y=C_1\cos x+C_2\sin x-\frac{1}{2}x^2\cos x+\frac{1}{2}x\sin x
    $$

    c. $y''+2y'+2y=e^x\sin x$ 

    :::info[Solution]

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    m^2+2m+2&=0\\
    m&=\frac{1}{2}[-2\pm\sqrt{4-8}]\\

    $$
    \begin{aligned}

    y_p=\text{Im}Ae^{(1+i)x}
    \end{aligned}

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    Y_p&=Ae^{(1+i)x} \\ 
    Y'_p&=(1+i)Ae^{(1+i)x} \\ 
    Y''_p&=2iAe^{(1+i)x}
    \end{aligned}

    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    2iAe^{(1+i)x}+2\left[(1+i)Ae^{(1+i)x}\right]+2\left[Ae^{(1+i)x}\right] &=e^{(1+i)x} \\

    \end{aligned}

    y_p&=\text{Im}Y_p=\frac{1}{8}e^x(-\cos x+\sin x)
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    $$
    \therefore y=e^{-x}(C_1\cos x+C_2\sin x)+\frac{1}{8}e^x(\sin x-\cos x)
    $$

    d. $y''-3y'+2y=x^2e^{3x}$

    $$
    y_p=e^{3x}(Ax^2+Bx+C)
    $$

    $$
    \begin{aligned}

    &=e^{3x}(3Ax^2+3Bx+3C+2Ax+B)\\

    \end{aligned}
